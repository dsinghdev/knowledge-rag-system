National Savings Certificates (VIIIth Issue) (NSC) - Scheme Details

(1) Who can open:-
(i) a single adult
(ii) Joint Account (up to 3 adults)
(iii) a guardian on behalf of minor or on behalf of person of unsound mind
(iv) a minor above 10 years in his own name.

(2) Deposit:-
(i) Minimum ₹ 1000/- and in multiple of ₹ 100/- No maximum limit.
(ii) Any number of accounts can be opened under the scheme.

(3) Interest:-
(i) 7.7 % per annum compounded annually but payable at maturity.
(ii) Deposits qualify for deduction under section 80C of Income Tax Act.

(4) Maturity:-
(i) The deposit shall mature on completion of five years from the date of the deposit.

(5) Pledging of account:-
(i) NSC may be pledged or transferred as security, by submitting prescribed application form at concerned Post Office supported with acceptance letter from the pledgee.
(ii) Pledging/transfer can be made to authorities like President of India/Governor of State, RBI/Scheduled Bank/Co-operative Society/Co-operative Bank, Corporation (Public/Private)/Local Authority/Government Company, Housing Finance Company.

(6) Premature closure:-
(i) NSC may not be prematurely closed before 5 years except the following conditions:-(a) On the death of a single holder, or any or all the holders in a joint account. (b) On forfeiture by a pledgee being a Gazetted Officer. (c) On order by court.

(7) Transfer of account from one person to another person:-
NSC may be transferred from one person to another person only on the following conditions:-(i) On the death of account holder to nominee/legal heirs. (ii) On the death of account holder to joint holder(s). (iii) On order by the court. (iv) On pledging to specified authorities.
